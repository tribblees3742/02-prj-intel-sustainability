Intel Timeline

From the first microprocessor to modern chip design and future trends, explore Intel's major milestones in innovation, sustainability, and computing power.

A journey from 1968 to the future

Intel's structured timeline highlights major milestones in chip design, sustainability, and new computing concepts.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore form Intel Corporation, ushering in the semiconductor era and starting Intel's mission to innovate computing.

1971

First Microprocessor

The Intel 4004 becomes the world's first commercial microprocessor, making programmable computers smaller and more accessible.

1978

8086 and x86 Architecture

The launch of the 8086 processor creates the x86 architecture, which becomes the foundation for modern PCs and servers.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces 32-bit computing with the 386 processor, enabling more powerful applications and better multitasking for personal computers.

2006

Rising Focus on Sustainability

Intel reaches its peak emissions and accelerates investments in energy efficiency, cleaner manufacturing, and renewable electricity.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches RISE—Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling—to align innovation with global sustainability and social goals.

2022

Net-Zero Commitment

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040, with a strong focus on renewable energy and low-carbon design.

2023

Modern Chip Design

New hybrid CPU and GPU architectures deliver higher performance while improving energy efficiency for AI, gaming, and professional workloads.

2024

Advanced Packaging & AI

Intel expands advanced packaging and AI-ready designs, using chiplets and system-level integration for scalable performance.

2030+

Future Trends

Intel is shaping future trends in sustainable silicon, AI-first computing, and energy-efficient data centers for the next generation of devices.

Hybrid Performance Design

Combining high-performance cores with efficiency cores in one package lets Intel deliver fast computing while saving power in everyday tasks.

AI-First Architecture

Intel's latest designs include hardware built for AI workloads, accelerating machine learning and enabling smarter applications.

Sustainable Manufacturing

New chip designs are paired with renewable energy use, water recycling, and waste reduction to support a cleaner manufacturing footprint.
